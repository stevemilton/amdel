Regulatory Landscape

The Framework Is Being
Built Right Now

This is not a future possibility. The FCA, Bank of England, and HM Treasury are actively building the UK stablecoin framework. The sandbox is open. The rules are being written.

Jun 2023

FSMA 2023 Royal Assent

Legal basis for bringing stablecoins into UK financial regulation.

May 2025

FCA CP25/14: Stablecoin Issuance Rules

Proposed rules for stablecoin issuance, custody, and prudential requirements. Defines authorisation, reserve backing, and redemption standards.

Nov 2025

FCA Regulatory Sandbox Open

Sandbox opened to stablecoin issuers. Live pathway for testing under FCA supervision.

Nov 2025

Bank of England: Systemic Stablecoins

Consultation on regime for sterling-denominated systemic stablecoins used in payments. Covers redemption at par, reserve requirements, and holding limits.

Dec 2025

Property (Digital Assets) Act 2025

Crypto assets confirmed as personal property under UK law. Legal certainty for tokenised assets.

2026

Final Rules Expected

FCA and Bank of England publish finalised stablecoin and cryptoasset regulations.

Oct 2027

Full UK Crypto Regime

Comprehensive cryptoasset framework takes effect. AMDEL aims to be operational and authorised before this date.

Infrastructure Stack

Built Like a Bank.
Deployed Like a Protocol.

A stablecoin is a financial system, not a software project. Every layer must meet institutional standards from day one.

01 Smart Contracts

ERC-20 with mint/burn, pause, freeze, and upgrade controls. At least two independent audits from reputable firms before mainnet deployment. Bug bounty post-launch.

02 Custody & Key Management

Multi-signature or MPC-based key management with role-based access. Institutional custody through a regulated provider (BitGo, Fireblocks, or equivalent). HSM-backed signing.

03 Reserve Management

GBP-L: short-duration UK Gilts and cash at FCA-authorised banks. AUR-L: LBMA-accredited London vaults. Segregated, bankruptcy-remote accounts.

04 Compliance Engine

KYC/AML for minting partners. Transaction monitoring. Sanctions screening (OFAC, UK HMT, EU). Travel Rule compliance. Full FCA record-keeping.

05 Banking & Payment Rails

UK Faster Payments and CHAPS for GBP on/off ramps. EMI partnerships for broad access. The banking relationship is the hardest component and the highest priority.

06 Transparency Dashboard

Public-facing: circulating supply, reserve composition, mint/burn activity, attestation history. A core trust mechanism and regulatory expectation.
